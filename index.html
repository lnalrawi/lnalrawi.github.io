Layth Nabeel AlRawi is a Global Technology Engineer - Data Science and Software, and a researcher specializing in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Human factors in industrial Informatics. With over 14 years in the oil and gas industry, he has held key roles at Weatherford, Nabors Industries, and Parker Wellbore, where he transitioned from field operations to digital innovation. He holds a Master’s degree in Computer Science from Çankaya University, focusing on human factors in energy systems. Currently, he is a PhD candidate at Klagenfurt University, researching interactive design, augmented reality (AR), and virtual reality (VR). Layth is passionate about bridging technology and human interaction to drive innovation.

My teaching and research interests are in the field of Human-Computer Interaction. Currently, my research focuses on interaction design and visual communication for 2D and 3D user interfaces in immersive technologies, as well as human factors in the energy industry.

New Research Project is Ongoing "HapTorq - Exploring New Frontiers in Industrial Innovation"

Posted on: Thursday, March 19th, 2025

Excited to be working on a new research project that bridges wearable technology and Torque Turn System! This initiative explores how real-time feedback can enhance operator awareness and efficiency in Tubular running services operations. The goal? To improve safety, precision, and user experience using cutting-edge human-computer interaction (HCI) principles. Looking forward to sharing insights and lessons learned along the way!
